Memorandum of Association (MOA): Core Object Areas

1) Research, Evidence, and Community Implementation

  • Public health and epidemiology research
  • Implementation and operational research
  • Evidence synthesis and policy translation
  • Field-based public health innovation

2) Research Support and Capacity Building

  • Protocol and study design support
  • Biostatistics and data services
  • Manuscript and publication support
  • Training, workshops, fellowships, internships

3) Public Health Programs and CSR Implementation

  • Program design and execution
  • Health promotion and prevention activities
  • Collaborations with government, academia, NGOs, and private partners

Incidental/Ancillary Objects

  • National and international collaboration
  • Receiving lawful grants, donations, and CSR funds
  • Publishing reports and educational materials
  • Conducting conferences and technical events

Articles of Association (AOA): Governance Framework

Membership

  • Initial members are MOA subscribers
  • New members require unanimous existing-member approval plus Board approval
  • No open or automatic membership

Board and Governance

  • Minimum two directors; up to fifteen as per law
  • Board manages operations under MOA/AOA
  • Director removal by special resolution

Founder/President Framework

  • Founder status is recognized as conceptual origin
  • President role is governance-advisory, not automatic Board voting authority
  • No automatic financial privilege from founder/president role

Divisional Structure (Operational)

Planned internal divisions include:

  • Research and Evidence
  • Public Health Implementation
  • Collaboration and Partnerships
  • Accounts and Legal Compliance
  • Software and Systems
  • Education and Fellowship
  • Monitoring, Evaluation, Impact, and Ethics
  • Grants, Funding, and Resource Mobilization

Application of Income and Winding Up

  • Income and property are applied only toward company objects
  • No dividend/bonus/profit distribution to members
  • On dissolution, assets transfer to a similar Section 8 entity, not members

Financial and Reporting Status

  • Annual reports: To be published after first reporting cycle
  • Audited statements: To be published after first statutory audit
  • Program utilization summaries: To be published after operations begin
